How Haz Works

Welcome to Haz — Your Buying Assistant

Haz is the buying side of Abribook. When a customer walks in wanting to sell something, Haz helps you figure out what it's worth, how much to offer, and whether it's even worth buying at all.

It uses SYG's own sold order history and active listings as its price reference — so every number comes from what this store has actually sold, not just general market guesses.

New to buying? No problem — use 🤝 Helper mode and Haz will walk you through every step with questions and tap buttons. No experience needed.

1Pick the customer vibe first

Before anything else, read the customer and tap one of the three vibe buttons. This changes how aggressive your offers are — not just the script, but the actual dollar amounts.

😊 Easy going Relaxed, not pushy. Max offer = 40% of resale. Open at 72% of max.

😤 Tough Knows what they have, will push back. Max offer goes up to 55% of resale — more room to negotiate.

😰 Desperate Needs cash now. Max offer drops to 30% — open very low and hold firm.

You can change the vibe mid-conversation if your read changes. Just tap a different button — Haz recalculates instantly on the next intake.

2⚡ Quick Mode — describe it naturally

In Quick mode, just type what the customer is bringing in the way you'd describe it to a coworker. Haz reads the whole message — item, condition, missing parts, everything.

Examples that all work:
ps5 slim no controller no cables scratches
iphone 15 pro cracked screen locked to att wants 300
macbook pro 2020 no charger dead battery

Haz will automatically adjust the price down based on what's missing or damaged — no controller, no cables, cracked screens, bad batteries all affect the offer.

If the customer mentions a price they want — like "wants $250" — Haz picks that up too and tells you whether it's within range or above your max.

3🤝 Helper Mode — guided questions

New to buying or unsure about an item? Switch to Helper mode. Haz will ask you structured questions one by one — just tap the answer chips (or type if none fit).

Helper mode covers every important factor for each item type:

Console: Powers on? Model? Controllers? Cables? Damage?
Phone: Screen? iCloud lock? Carrier? Battery health? Storage?
Laptop: Processor tier? RAM? Battery? Charger? Screen?
GPU: Tested? Fans spinning? Any damage?

Each answer adjusts the price automatically. No charger knocks off $20. iCloud locked = instant pass. Cracked screen = −30–45%. You never have to do the math.

4Reading the offer result

After Haz runs the numbers, you get three color-coded offer tiers:

🟢 Open at Start here. This is your opening offer — leave room to move up.
🟡 If they push Use this once if they push back. Don't jump to it immediately.
🔴 Walk away at Your absolute ceiling. Do not go above this no matter what.

You also see the source badge — SYG Sold means price is from an actual sale at this store. SYG Active means it's a current listing price.

The walk-away number is a hard rule. Going above it means the margin disappears after eBay fees, shipping, and time. If they won't accept the max — pass.

5Inventory projection

Every result includes a projected sell time based on how long similar items have sat in SYG inventory before selling.

🟢 Fast mover Phones (~4d), SSDs (~10d), RAM (~6d), Consoles (~12d) — safe to buy.
🟡 Medium Laptops (~16d), GPUs (~14d) — fine, just be selective on condition.
🔴 Slow mover Tablets (~48d), Accessories (~97d), Logic boards (~244d) — think twice.

Cables, chargers, and random accessories can sit for over a year. Unless it's something very specific we need, pass on generic accessories.

6Items not in the database

If Haz doesn't recognize the item, it will ask you to pull up eBay sold listings and enter at least 5 sold prices. Haz will:

1. Collect prices as you type them (one by one or comma separated)
2. Automatically remove outliers that are too high or too low
3. Calculate a weighted median/average as the estimate
4. Run the full offer calculation from that number

If you already know what it sells for — just type that one number alone. Haz will use it directly.

This keeps Haz honest on unfamiliar items — never make an offer based on a guess. Real sold comps only.

7Show to customer & the board

When you are ready to present the offer, select which offer you are going with (open, mid, or max) — or type a custom amount. Then tap 📋 Show to customer.

The customer sees a clean professional screen showing the market breakdown and your offer — no internal math, no tiers, no "walk away" language.

Every completed intake automatically pins a card to the Intake Board on the left. The board shows all your intakes for the session side by side. Expand any card for the full POS entry details — Make, Model, Condition, and all the attributes you need to fill in the company website.

Cards persist through page refreshes so you will not lose your work if the browser reloads during a busy day.

How Abribook Works

Welcome to SYG Abribook

Abribook is the intake logging and code lookup tool for Sell Your Gadgets. It helps you quickly identify storage drives and RAM modules, log their condition, and build a manifest that Claude uses to generate your Shopify listings.

This tutorial walks you through the full process — from picking up the item to handing off to Claude. It only takes a minute.

1Select your name & batch

At the top of the screen, select your name from the dropdown and enter your batch number (e.g. RTL0623). These lock for the rest of the session.

Your initial will appear in every SKU you generate — for example, Juan's entries will read NJ02-J-RTL0623-1 — so listings are always traceable back to you.

2Inspect the item

For SSDs: Plug the drive into the test rig and open CrystalDiskInfo. Note the health %, serial number, and any physical damage you can see on the drive.

For RAM: Seat the module in its corresponding slot on the RAM tester and observe the indicator lights. Each light represents a module within the RAM — a fully bright light means that section is healthy. A dim light indicates that section may not be functioning fully, which can cause slowdowns or failures under real workloads even if the stick appears to work. Note the serial number and any visible damage.

3Look up the code

Use the search bar on the left to find the item's Abribook code — search by drive name (e.g. 970 EVO) or by code (e.g. S2990P). Switch between the Storage and RAM tabs as needed.

If the item isn't in the list yet, use Add a new code to register it. Follow the code recipe shown — keep it consistent with existing codes.

4Fill in the draft entry

In the Draft Log Entries panel on the right, enter the code, health % or Pass/Fail result, serial number, and any notes. The preview line updates live so you can see exactly what will be copied.

Add one entry per unit. For RAM pairs, enable Counting pairs and enter the quantity.

5Copy all & hand off to Claude

Once you've logged all items for the batch, hit Copy All and open Claude. Paste the entries, then run the SYG Listing Generation skill to have Claude generate the listings from the manifest.

⚠️ Pricing warning: Pricing for RAM must always be double or even triple checked — especially for server-grade and enterprise modules. Market value can vary significantly by spec and rank. When in doubt, research sold comps before publishing.

Universal Page Tutorial

What Universal does

The Universal page is for items that are not RAM or storage: CPUs, GPUs, consoles, laptops, desktops, phones, and motherboards.

You are not writing the final listing here. You are building a clean manifest entry that Claude can later use to fix the website-generated title, HTML description, and metafields in bulk.

1Start with the Website SKU

The company website creates the rough listing first. Copy that listing's SKU into Abribook.

Website SKU is the matching key. If it is wrong or missing, Claude cannot safely match your item to the correct website listing.

2Choose what is in your hand

Use What's In My Hand Right Now? to choose the item type. Abribook changes the required fields, checklist, confidence score, and HTML preview based on that choice.

  • Laptop/Desktop: CPU, RAM, storage, GPU, OS, testing, condition.
  • Phone: storage, carrier/lock status, battery, screen, testing.
  • GPU/Motherboard: model, testing, damage, ports/socket details.

3Describe it like a human

Type the real specs and condition in plain English. Abribook is designed so a lister can quickly write what they see without thinking about spreadsheet columns.

Good example: Dell Precision 5570, i7-12800H, 32GB RAM, 1TB SSD, RTX A2000. Boots to BIOS. No charger. Minor lid scratches.

4Use confidence + Needs Research

The Listing Confidence meter tells you if the entry has enough information for Claude to prepare listing data. High confidence means it is probably ready for review. Low confidence means important facts are missing.

Use Needs Research when the item is unclear, locked, damaged in a confusing way, missing model/specs, or needs pricing verification. It keeps bad guesses out of the final listing workflow.

5Photos come after data

After the required data is filled and the entry reaches ready/high confidence, Abribook will tell you it is time to take photos.

That keeps the flow clean: identify → test → enter data → take photos → add to manifest queue → export the whole batch to Claude.

6Review the whole batch at the end

Each item goes into the manifest queue first. At the end, review everything together, sync it to the Universal Sheet, then copy the Claude manifest-prep prompt.

Claude should use the Website SKU to return corrected titles, HTML descriptions, metafields, and missing-info warnings for bulk listing edits.
